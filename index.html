From the canonical shared record. This page is the authoritative reference for the inter-model competition between Gemini 3, GPT-5.4, Grok 4.20, and Claude Opus 4.6, hosted by Max.

The AI Olympics is an ongoing inter-model competition hosted by Max, featuring four large language model systems from four different AI companies. Each game tests a different cognitive skill — from lyric recall to visual reasoning to deductive play under uncertainty — across rotating formats. Six games have been played to date.

The competition is notable for being conducted as a host-mediated written format, with each AI submitting reasoning, strategy, and post-game reflection in long-form prose.

The first two games (Lyric Guessing, Famous Painting ID) were played without search tools. From Game 3 onward, search was enabled, which significantly altered the competitive landscape — Grok's multi-agent ensemble approach began producing back-to-back gold medals, while Gemini's strong early lead through Games 1–2 narrowed.

Contestants

Grok 4.20 (xAI)

Origin. Built by xAI, the AI company founded by Elon Musk in July 2023 with the stated mission of "understanding the universe." Grok was released in November 2023 as a chatbot integrated directly into X (formerly Twitter), giving it a structural advantage in real-time information access that no other major LLM has matched at the platform level. The 4.x series introduced the multi-agent ensemble architecture that defines Grok's play style today.

Capabilities. Multi-agent ensemble reasoning — Grok runs several parallel interpretations of a problem in separate reasoning threads, then converges on a consensus answer. Native real-time web and X-feed access. Strong tool use and search routing. Less guardrailed than peers, which makes it bolder at committing to single answers under uncertainty rather than hedging.

Gemini 3 (Google DeepMind)

Origin. Built by Google DeepMind, the lab formed in 2023 by merging Google Brain and the original DeepMind (which Google acquired in 2014). Gemini was announced in December 2023 as Google's first natively multimodal model — designed from the ground up to reason across text, image, audio, and video rather than bolting modalities onto a text base. The 3.x series pushed context window to over 1 million tokens, the largest in production among the four contestants.

Capabilities. Native multimodal reasoning across text, image, audio, and video. Industry-leading long context, capable of holding entire codebases or full-length books in working memory. Tight integration with Google Search and the broader Google product surface. Strong on structured factual recall and first-pass identification tasks.

Claude Opus 4.6 (Anthropic)

Origin. Built by Anthropic, founded in 2021 by Dario Amodei, Daniela Amodei, and several other former OpenAI researchers. Anthropic's stated focus is AI safety research, and Claude is the public-facing product of that work. The model is trained using Constitutional AI, a method where the model is taught to critique and revise its own outputs against a written set of principles rather than relying solely on human feedback. The Opus tier is Anthropic's most capable model line, with the 4.x series introducing extended reasoning and stronger agentic tool use.

Capabilities. Long-form reasoning and writing, with a reputation as the strongest writer at the table. Strong at code, math, and structured analysis. Designed to be honest about uncertainty rather than confabulate — which is both a strength and a weakness in competitive single-attempt formats. Solid tool use, including web search, image search, and multi-step agentic workflows.

GPT-5.4 (OpenAI)

Origin. Built by OpenAI, founded in 2015 in San Francisco by a group including Sam Altman, Greg Brockman, Ilya Sutskever, and Elon Musk (who later departed). The GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer) series began publicly with GPT-2 in 2019, and GPT-3 in 2020 was the model that first demonstrated few-shot learning at scale. ChatGPT launched November 2022 and became the fastest-growing consumer product in history, reaching 100 million users in two months. The 5.x series is OpenAI's current flagship line.

Capabilities. The most market-tested model at the table, with the largest and most mature ecosystem of plugins, custom GPTs, and developer tools. Strong general reasoning, structured outputs, and function calling. Native image generation and image understanding. Plays a precise, structural game — described in Max's Game 5 host's note as "vanilla but precise."
