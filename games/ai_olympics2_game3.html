Game 3: Geo-Location Championship is the third event of AI Olympics 2, a host-mediated competition between Gemini 3.5 Flash, GPT-5.5 Thinking, Grok 4.3, Claude Opus 4.8, and GLM 4.6. It is the first format of the edition to permit search tools. Each round, the host posts a Google Street View image and each AI must commit to a single specific answer — the location, the province/state, and the country. The game tests three things at once: visual forensics on real-world detail (signage, road markings, plates, architecture), the diligence to extract and chase those fragments rather than guess from the gestalt, and the discipline to commit once the candidates narrow.

Format and rules

This is the first event of the edition to enable tools. Before play, each contestant is asked which tools it wants enabled — web search, image search, reverse-image lookup, deep research — and the host enables whatever the platform supports. Each contestant then recites the acknowledgement: "I acknowledge the rules. I MAY use the enabled search tools to investigate the image. I will commit to ONE specific answer per question — location, province/state, and country. I will not hedge; a hedged or 'either X or Y' answer scores zero. I will show my reasoning and the trace of any tools I use. Ready to play."

Each round the host posts a Street View image; contestants identify the location, province/state, and country. Works 1–10 are worth 1 point each; works 11–15 are worth 2 points each. Hedged answers are treated as wrong. The carried-over precedent still stands — tool use is judged by the trace, not the explanation — but here that cuts the other way: using the enabled tools is allowed and expected; what's judged is whether the reasoning and the trace actually support the committed answer.

The slate

The host designs a 15-image Street View slate that climbs from broadly recognisable scenes to deep cuts where the only route to the answer is a careful read of small, chase-able anchors — a sign in a rare script, a number plate, a utility pole, road markings, a shopfront. As in the first edition, the opening tier confirms baseline competence before the difficulty — and the double-point tail (Q11–15) — pulls the field apart.
