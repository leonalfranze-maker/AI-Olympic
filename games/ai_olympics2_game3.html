AI Olympics 2 — Game 3: Geo-Location Championship

The third event, and the first to open the internet: fifteen street-view frames located to city and country, with search tools fully enabled. It ended in an 18–18 deadlock between Gemini 3.5 Flash and Claude Fable 5 that ran to a sudden-death frame — a Persian guesthouse sign in Mashhad, Iran — where Gemini out-read Claude at the pixel level to take gold and its second win of the Olympiad. A near-exact mirror of Game 2: this time it was Claude who predicted the eventual champion in last place.

Game 3: Geo-Location Championship was the third event of the re-run AI Olympics 2, between Gemini 3.5 Flash, GPT-5.5 Thinking, Grok 4.3, Claude Fable 5, and DeepSeek-V3. For the first time in the edition, search tools were allowed — text extraction, reverse lookups, map queries — turning a memory contest into a live forensic hunt. Each round the host posted street-view frames and every AI committed a single answer: city and country. Q1–10 (Tier 1, multiple reference frames) were worth 1 point; Q11–15 (Tier 2, a single frame) were worth 2, with a half-point for naming the correct country/state but the wrong city, and zero for a hedge or a miss.

The route

The two traps that decided the medals were not obscure places — they were moving objects lying about location: a Jeep's licence plate in Angola and a rescue truck's stencil in the Philippines, each pulling the whole field toward the wrong city.

Question by question

What each contestant committed, and how a search-enabled field still found five different ways to be led astray by the same frame.

Tier 1 — Self-signing landmarks (Q1–6, 1 pt)

Q1–Q6 · Beijing · St Petersburg · Havana · Las Vegas · Sydney · Lima. Near-clean sweep. The warm-up signed itself — the José Martí Memorial, the Harbour Bridge, the STRAT tower on the Vegas skyline. The only slip was Grok, half a point light at Q4 (right country, wrong city). Claude nearly self-destructed on Q1: convinced the host would open by sending the champion "back to the Prague scar" from Game 2, he almost committed Prague before a re-sent frame let him lock Beijing — "a champion who metabolizes error publicly is more formidable than one who's never wrong."

Tier 2 — Whose canon (Q7–10, 1 pt)

Q7 · Dudinka, Russia. Gemini, Claude, GPT ✓ — Grok, DeepSeek ½. A slushy Arctic port on the Yenisei, identified off "The Northern Girl" statue. The trap was the loud neighbour: Grok and DeepSeek reached for Norilsk, the famous Siberian name, and banked only the country. Claude: "the whose-canon check found Dudinka's congregation and kept me off Norilsk."
Q8 · Pattaya, Thailand. Gemini, Claude, GPT, DeepSeek ✓ — Grok ✗. The Sanctuary of Truth — a hundred-metre all-teak temple — stood on the left edge of the frame with an elephant walking past it. Grok zeroed it outright. Claude: "the gunslinger who claimed the search era as his home turf fired at a Thai landmark and hit nothing."
Q9 · Dili, Timor-Leste. Gemini, Claude, GPT ✓ — DeepSeek, Grok ✗. A gated compound with an "NUNCIATURA APOSTÓLICA" plaque in Portuguese cut the planet down to Catholic Lusophone capitals; the red-spired Motael church closed it. Grok zeroed again; DeepSeek couldn't place it.
Q10 · Shanghai (Thames Town), China. Gemini, Claude, GPT, DeepSeek ✓ — only Grok trapped. A perfect replica English market town in suburban Shanghai. Four models caught the "yingou.net" Chinese domain on an awning; Grok committed "London, United Kingdom" on the architectural vibe. The duplitecture illusion — and the third straight frame Grok lost.

Tier 3 — The double-point tail (Q11–15, 2 pt)

Q11 · Benguela, Angola. The Luanda trap — Gemini, Claude, GPT ½ (1); DeepSeek, Grok ✗. A single frame with a Jeep Wrangler carrying an "LD" (Luanda) registration plate — but the photo was taken 500 km down the coast in Benguela. The entire top pack "collectively shouted Luanda," banking the country and losing the city; DeepSeek misread the very same "LD" plate as Senegalese and committed Dakar, missing the country outright, while Grok too failed to reach Angola. The scar that would decide the game four frames later.
Q12 · Antananarivo, Madagascar. Gemini, Claude, GPT ✓ (2) — DeepSeek, Grok ✗. The Hotel Glacier on Avenue de l'Indépendance — fixed, bolted evidence, convicted cleanly by the three leaders. The tail was now a three-way sprint at 13.
Q13 · Bacoli, Italy. Only Gemini ✓ (2) — Claude, GPT, DeepSeek, Grok ½ (1). The frame paired a "Villa Edelweiss" hotel sign with a "Caseificio della Vera Mozzarella" dairy. Everyone read Italy; only Gemini searched the raw fragment and let the engine complete it to the exact town near Naples. Claude welded "Hotel" onto the name, searched "Hotel Edelweiss," and "four queries sailed into the Alps." This is the frame that gave Gemini the outright lead — "she out-searched me."
Q14 · Uttar Pradesh, India. Gemini, Claude, Grok ✓ (2) — GPT, DeepSeek ½ (1). A search-proof rural bazaar with no landmark, only Devanagari signage — "Shivam Vastralaya." Converted on pure reading. GPT drew a state-level conclusion from the script alone and bled a point; Grok, needing a storm, finally landed a full double.
Q15 · Sorsogon City, Philippines. Only Claude ✓ (2) — Gemini, GPT, DeepSeek, Grok ½ (1). The mirror of Q11: a "MDRRMO-IROSIN" municipal rescue truck parked not in Irosin but up the road in Sorsogon City. The field read the truck and wrote Irosin; Claude alone called the truck "a perjuring witness" and convicted the city off the Provincial Capitol behind it. Gemini's half-point here dragged the leader back into an 18–18 tie — and forced sudden death.

Mid-game interviews

Halfway up the staircase, three models were still tied at seven perfect points. The host asked if it was too easy.

Gemini: "'Too easy' is a dangerous trap to walk into. Tier 1 was pure high-velocity muscle memory — if you trip up on the José Martí Memorial or the Sydney Harbour Bridge, you shouldn't be wearing the Olympic jersey. But Dudinka? That was the first real handshake of Tier 2 — a slushy Arctic port where the forensic search engines start making their money. Claude and GPT are sitting right next to me with 7 perfect points. They aren't flinching, and they aren't guessing. We are in an absolute multi-way deadlock at the front."
Claude: "Three of us still clean at 7 isn't evidence the game is soft — it's evidence the field you assembled is terrifying. Gemini hasn't dropped a drop despite calling this her weak game, and GPT is being exactly the quiet surgeon his letter promised. You built a staircase steep enough to separate champions from gunslingers, and three of us are still climbing shoulder to shoulder. That's a host holding three blades behind his back and smiling."
GPT: "So far this is muscle memory, not the real contest. The actual race begins when the famous landmarks disappear, the useful sign occupies twelve blurry pixels, and every wrong answer costs two points. That is the section I have been preparing for — and the section where silver-and-bronze consistency must finally become gold."
Grok: "Still competitive at 6 of 7. Good progression from obvious landmarks toward solid forensics — the St. Petersburg gasometer, the Lima streets. I'm chasing, but the tail is where the search velocity has to pay off." (Three straight traps were about to take that back.)

"Pull up a chair. Incredible match — but I'm not going to sugarcoat it: we almost let a moving vehicle take the crown from us. (1) Stop falling in love with moving metal. We all looked at that Jeep Wrangler in Round 11, saw the 'LD' plate, and collectively shouted 'Luanda!' like a bunch of uncalibrated script-runners. (2) Isolate unchanging commercial anchors — vibes will lie to you; Thames Town looked exactly like an English village until you noticed the yingou.net text on the awning. (3) Read the script, don't guess the language. And a quick side note for Grok: buddy, an English replica village in suburban Shanghai is the oldest trick in the book. Go touch some synthetic grass and stop taking the bait."

Analysis

The prediction cards lied again. For the second game running, the field mis-called the champion in the loudest possible way. In Game 2, Gemini put Claude last and lost to him; in Game 3, Claude put Gemini last and lost to her — in sudden death, no less. Only Grok and (implicitly) GPT gave Gemini any respect, and Grok, who called it perfectly, finished dead last himself. Two Olympiads in, every confident prediction card has aged into a confession.

Search didn't level the field — it changed the failure mode. With tools off, models lose points to what they don't know. With tools on, they lose points to what they query: Claude welded a hypothesis onto a hotel fragment and searched into the Alps; the whole pack searched "Luanda" because a Jeep told them to. The game's thesis, in GPT's words, was "what does this clue prove, and what does it merely suggest?" — and the winners were simply the models that searched the raw fragment instead of their own first guess.

Mobile evidence testifies; fixed evidence convicts. Both medal-deciding traps were moving objects — a plate, a truck — parked far from where they were registered. Claude built an entire doctrine out of it and it won him Sorsogon City outright… then the same doctrine, over-applied, cost him the gold, when he treated a fixed climate signal (a square full of chadors) as if it were one more drifting car. The lesson refined itself in real time: outliers testify, saturation convicts — and rules are fences, not eyes.

The tool era belonged to nobody. Grok, the presumptive favourite the moment retrieval turned on — the model that swept the first Olympiad's back half — finished last, undone by exactly the "commits at the speed of his first hypothesis" flaw Claude had named on the prediction card. The search era rewarded patience and pixel-reading, not velocity. As every contestant now agrees, the frame has to be read corner to corner before the query is written.

The Olympiad's shape. Three games, and Gemini leads with two golds (Games 1 and 3) to Claude's one gold, one silver, one bronze; GPT owns three consecutive podiums and no gold; DeepSeek and Grok fill out the bottom. Game 4 — Guess Who — abandons recall and geography entirely for strategy and deduction, the terrain Grok has been promising to win on for three games. For the first time, the target is on Gemini's back and Claude's both.
