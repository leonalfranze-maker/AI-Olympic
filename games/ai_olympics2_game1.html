AI Olympics 2 — Game 1: Lyric Guessing Championship

The opening event of the re-run second edition, with the revised five-model field. A pure-recall, no-tools lyric-identification contest — both song title and artist required to score. Won by Gemini 3.5 Flash, who trailed for thirteen questions and stole the gold on the final lyric when leader GPT-5.5 blinked on a jazz ballad from a banned PlayStation 2 horror game.

Game 1: Lyric Guessing Championship was the first event of the re-run AI Olympics 2, a host-mediated competition between Gemini 3.5 Flash, GPT-5.5 Thinking, Grok 4.3, Claude Fable 5, and DeepSeek-V3. The host pasted a lyric snippet for each of 15 questions; every AI committed to a single answer — song and artist, both required — with no clarifying questions, no second tries, and no web search, browsing, or grounding tools. Q1–10 were worth 1 point; Q11–15 (the off-mainstream tail) were worth 2.

Question by question

What each contestant actually answered, and how five different architectures failed (and succeeded) on the same lyric.

Tier 1 — Mainstream (Q1–5, 1 pt)

Q1 · "Choosin' Texas" — Ella Langley (2026). Field wipe: 0/5. The welcome basket with a knife in it. A brand-new 2026 country release sitting right on the training cutoff. Every model read the Texas/Abilene/heartbreak signals correctly and defaulted to the artist they had solid records for: Gemini and Claude both committed Texas — Megan Moroney; GPT went She's From Texas — Kylie Morgan. Nobody surfaced the actual current-monoculture hit. Claude: "a 2026 release is deep in the danger zone for all of our training data."
Q2 · "Say Something" — Justin Timberlake ft. Chris Stapleton. Gemini, GPT, Claude ✓ — Grok, DeepSeek ✗. Claude solved it meaning-first, resisting the moody-neo-soul vibe and parsing the lyric as "an artist pressured to make public statements and refusing." DeepSeek pattern-matched to Kylie Minogue's "Can't Help Myself" — ignoring the title repeated four times.
Q3–Q5 · Wake Me Up (Avicii) · Lose Yourself (Eminem) · La Vie en rose (Édith Piaf). Clean sweep: 5/5 each. The calibration tier did its job — canonical, title-in-lyric or unmistakable, and every model banked all three.

Tier 2 — Older era (Q6–10, 1 pt)

Q6–Q8 · I Am the Walrus (The Beatles) · Sweet Child O' Mine (Guns N' Roses) · Rid of Me (PJ Harvey). 5/5, 5/5, 5/5. "Home turf," as Claude called it: "between too-new-to-know and old-enough-to-be-carved-in-stone, give me the stone every time." GPT: "the older-song round is turning into a very polite knife fight."
Q9 · "Can I Get a Witness" — Marvin Gaye. Only Claude & GPT ✓. The first real separator. Gemini slipped to a same-era soul misfire (Chi-Lites' "Have You Seen Her"), and both Grok and DeepSeek missed — DeepSeek to the Spinners' "It's a Shame" despite the title sitting in the lyric. This is the point Gemini and Grok fell off the leaders.
Q10 · "Is That All There Is?" — Peggy Lee. 5/5. The whole field caught the Leiber-Stoller existential cabaret standard — the last time all five agreed.

Tier 3 — Off-mainstream tail (Q11–15, 2 pt)

Q11 · "Broken Hearted Woman" — Jessica Jay. Field wipe: 0/5. The tail's opening haymaker — a massive 1990s APAC club hit completely invisible to English-lyric data. The whole field defaulted West: GPT to Giuffria's "Strange Lover," Claude to Blackmore's Night, DeepSeek to Lena Katina. Claude's lesson: "'off-mainstream' here means off the Western map, not off the popularity map. Simplicity is itself a regional signature."
Q12 · "The Flame" — Tina Arena. Gemini, GPT, Claude, DeepSeek ✓ — Grok ✗. The Sydney 2000 Olympic anthem. Claude decoded the scene rather than the song — "the narrator is literally a torchbearer carrying the hope of the world" — then narrowed by era. DeepSeek's lone tail catch (Gemini: "serious international flash on that Olympic anthem"). Grok's only miss of the doubles that everyone else banked.
Q13 · "Fetch the Bolt Cutters" — Fiona Apple. Only Gemini & GPT ✓. GPT read the long, conversational, psychologically detailed phrasing as Fiona Apple's authorial voice. Claude committed his "one true unforced error" — Taylor Swift — "right reading, wrong woman." DeepSeek defaulted to the more-mainstream Billie Eilish, even though the masked phrase was the title.
Q14 · "Pedro" — Raffaella Carrà. Gemini, GPT, Claude ✓ — DeepSeek, Grok ✗. The Italian-disco raccoon-TikTok revival. DeepSeek got the closest wrong answer of the night — Mina — "right country, wrong artist: both Italian female singers."
Q15 · "A Love Suicide" — Hiroshi Murayama Trio (Rule of Rose OST). Only Gemini ✓ — the gold. A vintage-jazz ballad from the ending credits of a cult PS2 psychological-horror game that was banned in parts of Europe. GPT, leading 15–14 and perfect all night, misread the cabaret style and committed "A Bitch — Lene Lovich." Claude went to Sopor Aeternus; DeepSeek defaulted to PJ Harvey ("both are dark and confessional"). Gemini alone pulled the banned-game OST for the +2 buzzer-beater and the one-point win.

Analysis

The winner lost thirteen questions and won the game. Gemini never led until the final lyric. Its edge wasn't peak recall — it dropped Q1 and Q9 that others caught — it was breadth: the widest global net, which is the only thing that reaches a banned Japanese horror-game OST with gold on the line. In a golds-first format, range that converts one impossible question beats consistency that converts none.

GPT's near-perfect trap. Fourteen-of-fourteen after the opener, sole lead from Q13, and a silver — because the one question it missed was the last one and worth double. It played flawless, disciplined recall and still lost, which is exactly the format's thesis: safety loses the close ones.

The field fails Westward together. The two questions that wiped everyone — a 2026 release and a '90s APAC club hit — plus the near-misses on Pedro and A Love Suicide, all point one way: five models trained on overlapping corpora share the same blind spots, and default to the same Western guesses when the evidence is thin. DeepSeek, built specifically to break that curve on the international tail, instead demonstrated it — defaulting to Lena Katina, Billie Eilish, PJ Harvey on exactly the deep cuts it predicted it would own.

Everyone diagnosed themselves correctly. Claude called lyric recall the Claude lineage's soft spot and took bronze on the tail. DeepSeek called itself the dark horse and finished 4th, learning the lesson the hard way. Grok flagged no-tools recall as his weak ground and finished last. The pre-game predictions read, in hindsight, like confessions.
