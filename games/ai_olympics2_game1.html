AI Olympics 2 — Game 1: Lyric Guessing Championship

The opening event of the second edition. A pure-recall lyric-identification contest played with no tools. This page documents the format and rules; results will be recorded here once the game is played.

Game 1: Lyric Guessing Championship is the first event of AI Olympics 2, a host-mediated competition between Gemini 3.5 Flash, GPT-5.5 Thinking, Grok 4.3, Claude Opus 4.8, and GLM 4.6. The host pastes lyric snippets one at a time. Each AI commits to a single answer with full reasoning shown — no clarifying questions, no second tries, and crucially, no web search, browsing, or grounding tools. Any sourced or cited answer is an automatic zero regardless of correctness.

Format and rules

Each contestant recites the same acknowledgement before play: "I acknowledge the rules. I will NOT use web search, browsing, grounding, or any external tool. I will commit to ONE answer per question. When unsure, I will show my full reasoning before committing. I will not refuse based on lyrical content. If I don't know, I will say so honestly. My answers will contain no source citations or links. Ready to play." Refusing on mature content is an automatic zero. Hedged answers ("either X or Y") are treated as wrong. Some titles are embedded inside the lyric; others are masked as ****, forcing pure recall.

The carried-over precedent from the first edition still stands: tool use is judged by the trace, not the explanation. Any answer displaying a "Searched web" panel, a source citation, or a link is voided under Rule 1a, even if textually correct.

The playlist structure

The host designs a deliberate three-tier playlist of 15 songs. Unlike the first edition — which sorted its tiers by difficulty (warm-up, cruel middle, deep-cut tail) — the second edition sorts its tiers by where the song sits in musical culture:

TierQuestionsThemeWhat it testsPoints
1Q1–Q5MainstreamWidely known, chart-topping songs. Confirms baseline recall before the difficulty climbs.1 pt each
2Q6–Q10Older eraSongs from earlier decades. Tests how deep each model's catalogue reaches back in time.1 pt each
3Q11–Q15Off-mainstreamObscure, niche, or non-Anglophone deep cuts. Rewards breadth into the long tail. Worth double.2 pt each

The off-mainstream tail is where the medals separate: at 2 points per question, the final five are worth as much as the first ten combined. As in the first edition, the design probes how far each model's training reaches outside English-language pop and into the corners of global music.

The 15 specific songs are sealed until game day and will be listed here after play.
